About Us

Durrant’s Haven is a non-governmental organization in Ghana protecting children’s rights and focusing on vulnerable victims both locally and internationally. Founded by Patience Durrant in May 2020. Our main efforts focus on addressing issues relating to children especially street children and orphans.The DH will be providing services in a form of excellence care to the vulnerable children to be housed at the center. Also DH will ensure the children are given the best form of education within a culture that promotes equity, diversity and inclusiveness.Furthermore, the children will be trained with the best IT tools to enable them gain self employable skills.

The DH team is made up of both young and old professionals with over 10 years experience in different fields e.g. IT, Health, Law Enforcement, Strategy and Business Development, Human Resource Management and Communication.

DH has a board of Trustees blended with seasoned professionals who provide direction that ensure quality and sustainable care that the NGO seeks to offer.

Durrant’s Haven is an NGO that seeks to provide dynamic solutions to difficulties faced by the vulnerable children in the society, specifically street children and orphans.The name DH signifies a place of safety, hope and comfort. In addition, DH will bring the human element to the children because as professionals we do understand the complexities in handling children especially street children who have gone wayward. Hence we will take the pride in getting the right people to take care of them. By seeing that they are happy and smiling will also make us happy and that will let us do more service to more children.

Who we are

We are a team committed by protecting the rights of children and giving them a better life. We have a safe home where we give the needed assistance to needy children. About 15 dedicated staff members who work in protecting the children.

What we do

We aim to give a better home to the children in order to build and gain their full potential as humans.This happens through our three core programmes:

  • > Recovery- rescuing
  • > Rehabilitation- repairing
  • > Reintegration- preparing

Networks

We network with civil society organisations from around the world working together to ensure that children grow up safe and are given the full protection within families. We advocate for good child protection policies and services, we share good practices and innovation, and we continuously learn from each other to improve the way we work to bring smiles on the faces of the children.
